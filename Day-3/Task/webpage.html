About Eminem

Early Life

Born on October 17, 1972, in St. Joseph, Missouri, Marshall Bruce Mathers III faced a turbulent and impoverished childhood. Moving frequently between Missouri and the suburbs of Detroit, Michigan, he struggled with poverty, bullying, and a difficult family dynamic. Seeking an escape, Mathers found solace in hip-hop at a young age. By the time he was fourteen, he was immersed in the Detroit rap scene, frequently attending open mics and cyphers. He eventually dropped out of high school, taking up various minimum-wage jobs while relentlessly pursuing a career in music..

The Underground Hustle and "Infinite"

In 1992, Eminem joined a succession of ad-hoc Detroit groups, including Basement Productions, the New Jacks, and eventually D12 (the Dirty Dozen). In 1996, he released his first independent solo album, Infinite, through Web Entertainment. The album struggled to make an impact or gain commercial traction, and many local critics dismissed his style. Facing poverty and personal setbacks, including an eviction, Mathers channeled his frustrations into creating his aggressive alter ego, Slim Shady. This dark, satirical, and highly technical persona became the foundation for his breakout.
