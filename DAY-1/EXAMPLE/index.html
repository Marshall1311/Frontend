Yo! eminem

I don't know when admiration became addiction. At first, you were just another voice in my headphones. When the world got too loud, your music was the only thing that made sense. Every lyric felt like you had stolen pages from my diary. It felt impossible that someone I'd never met could understand me better than the people sitting next to me every day. So I started talking to you. Not out loud. Just through messages I never sent, letters I never finished, conversations I replayed in my head. I kept thinking, *If you knew I existed, maybe things would be different.* Every day I checked my phone. Every notification made my heart race. Every silence felt personal. Maybe you were busy. Maybe you never saw it. Maybe I just wasn't worth noticing. That's the sentence that kept growing. I stopped expecting people around me to understand. Instead, I built a world where one reply from you could fix everything. I gave you a job you never asked for—to save someone you'd never met. The funny thing is, you never promised me anything. You only made music. I was the one who turned songs into conversations and verses into promises. One night I looked in the mirror and realized I didn't miss *you*. I missed being understood. There's a difference. You weren't the answer. You were just the spark that reminded me someone else had survived their own darkness. Maybe that's what music is supposed to do. Not carry us forever. Just help us walk until we can carry ourselves. So today, I'm writing this for the last time. Not because I stopped respecting you. Because I finally stopped waiting for someone else to write the ending of my story.

Dear future me

If you're reading this, it means time kept its promise. Right now I'm sitting in a room that feels too small for the dreams inside it. The speakers are cheap. The microphone isn't perfect. Some nights the silence is louder than the music. But today something happened. I heard a song. Not because it had the biggest beat or the catchiest hook, but because it sounded honest. It wasn't pretending to be fearless. It admitted fear. It admitted failure. It admitted that chasing a dream sometimes feels like building a castle out of sand. That song made me realize something. Every artist people admire was once unknown. Every legend once questioned whether anyone would ever hear them. The difference wasn't talent alone—it was that they kept writing when nobody was listening. So this is my promise. I won't measure my worth by streams, views, or applause. I'll measure it by whether I told the truth every time I picked up a pen. Maybe today I'm broke. Maybe tomorrow I'll still be invisible. Maybe people will laugh at the songs I spend sleepless nights creating. But one day someone might press play because they need hope. Maybe they'll hear a line I wrote and decide not to quit. If that happens even once, every lonely night will have meant something. I don't want fame if it costs who I am. I don't want success that leaves my soul behind. I want music that survives after the speakers go quiet. If I ever make it, I hope I remember these days—the cheap headphones, the unfinished verses, the doubts that followed me everywhere. Because these are the pages that give the ending meaning. And if I don't make it... Then at least I'll know I lived honestly. I created instead of wondering what could have been. I chose courage over comfort. To whoever finds this—whether it's the future version of me or someone chasing a dream of their own— Keep writing. Keep learning. Study the people who came before you, not to become copies of them, but to understand why their words lasted. The world doesn't need another imitation. It needs your story. So when everything feels impossible, write another verse. When nobody believes in you, practice another hour. When life tells you to quit, answer with another song. One day these empty pages may become someone's reason to keep going. Until then, I'll keep building.
